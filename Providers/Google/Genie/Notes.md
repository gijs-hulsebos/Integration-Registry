## ⚖️ {{Pros & Cons}}

** {{What is great about it?}} **
* {{1. Action-Controllable: Unlike video generators (Sora/Veo), you can actually 'play' the output. You can jump, walk, and interact with objects as if it’s a game.}}
* {{2. Emerging Physics: The model has 'learned' how gravity and collisions work just by watching videos, meaning water flows and objects fall without a traditional game engine.}}
* {{3. World Sketching: You can upload a simple drawing or a photo, and Genie 3 transforms it into a navigable environment immediately.}}
* {{4. Real-time Generation: It generates the world 'on-the-fly' as you move, meaning the world expands infinitely based on your actions.}}

* ** {{What sucks / limitations?}} **
* {{1. Session Limits: As of March 2026, world generation is capped at roughly 60 seconds per session before the 'memory' of the world starts to degrade.}}
* {{2. Visual Fidelity: The resolution is currently locked at ~720p at 24fps, which feels more like a dream or a retro game than high-end CGI.}}
* {{3. Regional Lock: Strictly available for US-based Google AI Ultra accounts (VPN required for EU access).}}

## 🧠 {{My Notes & Learnings}}
* **Watch out for:** {{'World Melting'—if you move too fast or perform erratic actions, the environment can hallucinate and change structure (e.g., a floor becoming a wall).}}
* **Tips:** {{Use it in combination with 'Nano Banana Pro' for the best initial frame quality. A high-quality starting image results in a much more stable playable world.}}
* **Pricing feeling:** {{Very expensive. It’s part of the $249/month 'Google AI Ultra' business/pro plan, though a 'lite' preview is rumored for standard Gemini Advanced users soon.}}
